Predicting Medical Insurance Costs

Regression Modeling and Cost Analysis

Analyzed a medical insurance dataset to understand how demographic and lifestyle factors influence healthcare costs. Built and evaluated multiple regression models, identifying smoking and BMI as the dominant cost drivers.

The final model incorporated an interaction between BMI and smoking and achieved an adjusted R² of 0.84, illustrating how lifestyle factors compound financial risk in healthcare.

Missing Data, Imputation, and Policy-Relevant Analysis

Research Fellow, Global Research Institute

Evaluated how missing-data imputation affects global healthcare expenditure modeling using World Bank data from 211 economies (2000–2025). Compared traditional and machine-learning-based imputation methods using RMSE and R², showing that advanced approaches such as random forest and MICE improve predictive accuracy and explanatory power in cross-country health financing analysis.

Key analysis scripts (R):

  • 01_global_health_data_cleaning.R — data cleaning, indicator mapping, missingness diagnostics, and MCAR testing
  • 02_global_health_imputation_comparison.R — comparison of mean, KNN, regression, MICE, and random forest imputation with model evaluation
